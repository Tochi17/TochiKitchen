---Red Velvet Cake---

Ingredients

380g Self-Raising Flour
250g Granulated Sugar
115g Butter
4 Eggs (Room Temperature)
240ml Buttermilk
240ml Vegetable Oil
10g Cocoa Powder
1 Teaspoon Baking Powder
1 Tablespoon Vanilla Extract
1 Teaspoon White Vinegar
Red Food Colouring

Cream Cheese Frosting

450g Cream Cheese
170g Butter
660g Icing Sugar
1 1/2 Teaspoon Vanilla


Extra Ingredients

Double Cream, 340g Icing Sugar, 150g Butter, Oreos, Kinder, Smarties, Milk Chocolate, Sprinkles

Also:

If you want to decorate your cake like this or somthing similar the instructions are down below.
It is a 2 tier, 8 inch cake so you will need to double the batches and make 2 cakes.

Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Grease two 10-inch cake pans and line with parchment paper.
2. Whisk the flour, baking soda, cocoa powder, and salt together in a large bowl. Set aside.
3. Beat the butter and sugar together on medium-high speed until combined, about 1 minute.
4.Scrape down the sides and up the bottom of the bowl with a rubber spatula as needed.
5.Add the oil, egg yolks, vanilla extract, and vinegar and beat on high for 2 minutes. (Set the egg whites aside.)
6.Scrape down the sides and up the bottom of the bowl with a rubber spatula as needed.
7. On low speed, add the dry ingredients in 2-3 additions alternating with the buttermilk. Beat in your desired amount of food coloring until combined.
8.Whisk or beat in the 4 egg whites on high speed until fluffy peaks form for about 3 minutes. Gently fold into cake batter. The batter will be silky and slightly thick.
9. Divide batter between cake pans. Bake for 30-32 minutes or until the tops of the cakes spring back when a toothpick poked in the center comes out clean.
10.Remove cakes from the oven and cool completely in the pans set on a wire rack. The cakes must be completely cool before frosting and assembling.

11. Make the frosting: In a large bowl using a handheld or stand mixer with a whisk or paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and butter together on medium-high speed until smooth, about 2 minutes. Add the confectioners’ sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Beat on low speed for 30 seconds, then increase to high speed and beat for 3 minutes until completely combined and creamy. Add more confectioners’ sugar if frosting is too thin or an extra pinch of salt if frosting is too sweet. Frosting should be soft, but not runny.
12. Stacking and frost: Using a large knife, slice a thin layer off the tops of the cakes to create a flat surface. Place 1 cake layer on your cake stand or serving plate. Evenly cover the top with frosting. Top with 2nd layer and spread remaining frosting all over the top and sides. I always use an icing spatula for the frosting.

Further Instructions

1. Beat together the butter and icing sugar on a medium/high setting on the mixer. Add the vanilla and double cream after it is combined and mix again.
2. Coat the whole cake in buttercream making sure it is coated evenly.
3. In a bowl heat the double cream for 45 seconds and mix with milk chocolate untip melted. Pour this ontop of the cake making drips as you go.
4. Start sticking the different chocolates and oreos ontop and around the cake.
5. If you like a some pearls or any other edidle decorations you want.
6. Use a grater and grate some chocolate and this will be to stick around the bottom of the cake.
7. Roll out a small chunk of fondant and cut out the words "Happy birthday" or anything you desire.
